Feature summaries below are intentionally concise and oriented around product/job-to-be-done rather than implementation internals.

## Executive Summary

- Rove is ahead when the comparison is about runtime substrate: daemon-centered control, immutable node identity, signed remote execution, approvals, secret backends, versioned config/specs, extension provenance, and converged CLI/API/WebUI control.

- Rove is behind when the comparison is about product surface area: consumer polish, channel breadth, mobile shells, domain workflow packs, deployment bundles, and ecosystem/tutorial density.

- The replace-all opportunity is not to clone each repo. It is to make Rove the secure agent/workflow/channel/node substrate that can absorb the useful pieces of those repos as specs, templates, channels, packs, and operator surfaces.


## Category Read

- Personal assistants: Rove lags on ready-to-use chat/mobile/voice polish, but leads on infra correctness and mesh/security direction.

- Agent-company systems: Rove lags on multi-agent orchestration UX, but leads on trust/transport/config foundations.

- Research and creator packs: Rove lags on packaged domain workflows, but leads as a reusable substrate for turning those packs into structured agents/workflows.

- Skills, memory, and tool ecosystems: Rove lags on ecosystem density and specialized memory/router depth, but leads on signed catalog trust and runtime enforcement.

- Channels and ops: Rove lags heavily on breadth and polished operations dashboards, though Telegram and the command center now make the direction credible.
